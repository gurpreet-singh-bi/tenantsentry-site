Landlord ERPs & Property Platforms

Re-Leased · Cirrus8 · Rex · Property Tree · Yardi

These systems automate rent collection, outgoings billing, and landlord reporting. They're excellent — for landlords. They have zero financial incentive to flag an invoice error in your favour.

These tools abstract your lease clauses for AASB 16 compliance. They know what your lease says — but they never ingest your invoices to check whether you're actually being billed correctly.

Manual, costly & one-time only

The structural reason

Why hasn't someone
already built this?

It's not an oversight. There are structural conflicts of interest that prevent the incumbents from ever building what TenantSentry does.

🏢

Re-Leased & Cirrus8 can't

Their paying customers are landlords and property managers. A tenant-side auditing tool would flag errors that directly reduce their clients' revenue. They'd lose their entire customer base to build it.

📊

Accurait & LeaseEagle won't

They sell to corporate controllers who need clean balance sheets for auditors. That buyer doesn't care about clawing back $8,000 in illegal land tax — they care about AASB 16 compliance. Different problem entirely.

🔍

Boutique auditors can't scale

They have the tenant-side alignment but not the software engineering capability. Their business model depends on high-value, low-volume engagements — not a $399/lease automated audit accessible to every SME.
