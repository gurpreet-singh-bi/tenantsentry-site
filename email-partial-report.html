Rent Review  ·  Clause 12.3
Ratchet clause — rent cannot decrease at any market review
Clause 12.3 contains a "greater of current or market" mechanic. Rent can only move upward at each review, even if Perth commercial rents fall significantly. This clause is enforceable under the Commercial Tenancy (Retail Shops) Agreements Act 1985 (WA) and is extremely difficult to remove once a lease is executed.
HIGH
Outgoings  ·  Schedule B, Items 4–9
Capital works misclassified as recoverable outgoings
Schedule B, Items 4–9 include HVAC replacement, roof membrane, and structural façade — all capital expenditure items. These cannot lawfully be passed to tenants as outgoings without specific disclosure in WA. Estimated misclassification risk flagged for full audit quantification.
HIGH
Option to Renew  ·  Clause 31.1
Option notice window is 3 months — missing it forfeits renewal entirely
Clause 31.1 requires written notice no later than 3 months before lease expiry. WA standard practice is 6–9 months. On a 20-year lease, this is a single, easily-missed deadline — with no relief or recourse once passed.
