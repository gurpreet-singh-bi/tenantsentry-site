Our story

We got tired of paying for our landlord's mistakes.

So we built the tool we wished we'd had.

TenantSentry started with a bill that didn't add up. A landlord invoice with land tax buried in the outgoings — illegal under Victorian law, but easy to miss if you don't know where to look. Most tenants never check. We did, and we found the same pattern across dozens of leases.

We got sick of seeing local businesses get overcharged on their outgoings, so we built an Australian tool to fight back. 100% Australian owned and operated — because this problem is uniquely ours, shaped by our state tenancy laws, our lease structures, and our property market.

The problem isn't bad landlords. It's information asymmetry. Landlords have property management systems, in-house accountants, and decades of billing practice on their side. Tenants have a 60-page lease PDF and a gut feeling that something's off.

We built TenantSentry to close that gap — combining forensic audit expertise, state legislation knowledge, and AI to give every commercial tenant the same firepower that used to cost $10,000 and six weeks with a solicitor.

How we work.

Always on the tenant's side

We have no relationship with landlords, property managers, or real estate groups. Our only incentive is finding what you're owed.

Transparent, not black-box

Every finding is cited to the exact lease clause and legislation that supports it. You can verify every conclusion yourself.

You stay in control

We surface findings and draft letters. You decide what to send. We never act on your behalf without your explicit approval.

Built for SMEs, not just enterprise

A single-site café owner deserves the same audit quality as a 50-site pharmacy chain. That's why we charge per lease, not per headcount.
